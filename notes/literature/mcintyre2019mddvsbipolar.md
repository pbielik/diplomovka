## Kľúčové evidenčné bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: opening rationale
- locator: PDF p. 1 / article p. 15, opening paragraphs
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Given the similarity in clinical presentation between major depressive disorder (MDD) and the depressive episodes of bipolar disorder (BP), it is inevitable that diagnostic errors will occur. Both overdiagnosis and underdiagnosis of BP are all too common, and it may take a decade or longer to reach a correct diagnosis. ... The purpose of this article is to provide psychiatrists and primary care clinicians with a set of best practices to improve their ability to make an accurate differential diagnosis between MDD and BP."
- parafráza: Paper hneď otvára differential diagnosis ako reálny klinický problém s častými chybami a dlhým diagnostickým oneskorením. To je silná opora pre to, prečo treba v interview citlivo pracovať aj s differential-diagnosis vrstvou.
- use: klinické zdôvodnenie významu differential diagnosis

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: poll/focus-group synthesis
- locator: PDF p. 2 / article p. 16, Figure 2 + surrounding text
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The online poll response to this question was fairly evenly distributed, with an accurate family history leading the way, followed by corroborating evidence from family, clinical presentation, and finally, evidence of early age at onset of depressive symptoms. ... The expert panel agreed that both the vocabulary used to describe psychiatric disorders and the reliability of the medical history of family members should be regarded with skepticism although not discounted."
- parafráza: Článok ukazuje, že klinici pri rozlišovaní MDD a BP najviac spoliehajú na rodinnú anamnézu, corroboration od rodiny, klinický obraz a vek nástupu. Zároveň ale upozorňuje, že family history sa nemá brať nekriticky.
- use: opora pre to, aké klinické informácie majú byť v rozhovore cieľavedome zbierané

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: section `The Role of Screening Instruments`
- locator: PDF p. 4-5 / article p. 18-19, `The Role of Screening Instruments`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The MDQ is one of several self-administered instruments developed to improve detection of BP. It was intended to be used as a screening instrument, not as the diagnostic measure. Unfortunately, some clinicians regard a positive screen result in the MDQ as a presumptive diagnosis. ... Because screening scales for BP have modest sensitivity of around 65%, clinicians risk missing the diagnosis of BP in one-third of patients if they inquire about BP only in patients who screen positive."
- parafráza: McIntyre výslovne varuje, že screening tools ako MDQ sú len screening, nie diagnóza. Pri ich izolovanom použití hrozí, že sa BP prehliadne približne u tretiny pacientov.
- use: keď budeš potrebovať argument proti redukcii diferenciálnej diagnostiky na jednoduchý self-report screen

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: sections on psychiatric comorbidity and substance-related overlap
- locator: PDF p. 6-7 / article p. 20-21, Figures 4-5 + surrounding text
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Comorbidity of MDD and BP with other psychiatric disorders is common; thus, a comprehensive evaluation is necessary to identify any comorbid conditions. Comorbidity complicates the differential diagnosis due to overlapping symptoms that may mistakenly be attributed to mania or hypomania. ... If a patient is depressed and using or abusing a substance ... the psychomotor agitation occurring as part of an intoxication or withdrawal syndrome may mimic mania, potentially contributing to misdiagnosis of BP."
- parafráza: Diferenciálnu diagnostiku komplikuje vysoká psychiatrická komorbidita a aj substance-related stavy, ktoré môžu napodobňovať manické alebo hypomanické symptómy. Preto paper odporúča komplexné hodnotenie, nie rýchle uzavretie diagnózy.
- use: obhájenie, prečo má kvalitný rozhovor zachytávať aj komorbidity a kontext užívania látok

### Blok 5
- opiera sa o: section `Use of staging models ...` + prodromal discussion
- locator: PDF p. 8-9 / article p. 22-23, staging/prodrome sections
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "It is advantageous to cast a wider net of diagnostic possibilities first and then narrow down the list as more data are collected. ... If a diagnosis is particularly challenging, it is critical to see the patient more often and to remain in contact with the family to gain consistent feedback and insight. ... It is important to remember that psychiatric differential diagnoses often cannot be made cross-sectionally and require monitoring of the course over time to lend credence to one suspected diagnosis versus another."
- parafráza: Paper odporúča nezačínať úzkou hypotézou, ale širšou sieťou možností, ktorú klinik zužuje až s ďalšími dátami. Dôležitý je follow-up, kontakt s rodinou a sledovanie priebehu v čase.
- use: metodická a klinická opora pre longitudinal thinking pri differential diagnosis

### Blok 6
- opiera sa o: `Clinical Recommendations`
- locator: PDF p. 10 / article p. 24, `Clinical Recommendations`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Screen all patients for BP at first visit and subsequent visits if there are insufficient treatment outcomes. ... Family history may point more to a unipolar or a bipolar diathesis. ... Screening tools do not diagnose or make a differential diagnosis, but are a part of the initial interview. ... Course of illness over time is the 'great validator' of a suspected diagnosis."
- parafráza: Záverečné odporúčania paperu kondenzujú differential-diagnosis prax: screenovať, ale nespoliehať sa len na screen; pýtať sa na family history a sledovať longitudinal course, ktorý je napokon najsilnejší validátor podozrenia.
- use: veľmi praktický klinický anchor pre final framing differential diagnosis

## Kľúčová definícia / výsledok / argument
- Najspoľahlivejší diferenciálno-diagnostický postup medzi MDD a BP je viaczdrojový a longitudinálny: klinický rozhovor, family history, screening ako pomocný nástroj, komorbidity, substances a priebeh ochorenia v čase.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Ide o expert-based clinical highlights, nie o systematický review.
- Paper je veľmi užitočný na klinickú prax a heuristiky, ale konkrétne percentá a odporúčania treba čítať ako practice-oriented synthesis, nie ako finálnu diagnostickú autoritu.
