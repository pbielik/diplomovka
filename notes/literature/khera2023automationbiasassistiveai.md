## Klucove evidencne bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: opening editorial framing
- locator: PDF p. 1, opening paragraphs
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "Most AI algorithms are designed to be assistive technologies - augmenting, not replacing, clinicians' decision-making."
  >
  > "AI models are imperfect and lack the broader clinical context that may be relevant for patient care."
  >
  > "clinicians may be unsure of whether or when to make a decision that runs counter to a recommendation"
- parafraza: Paper nastavuje zakladny eticky ramec velmi presne: asistivna AI nema nahradzat klinika, ale ani pritomnost klinika negarantuje bezpecie, ak model neobsiahne kontext a clovek nevie, kedy odporovaniu modelu doverovat.
- use: silny uvodny anchor pre human oversight a limit asistivnych AI systemov

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: explainability critique
- locator: PDF p. 1-2, pasaz od `To address this...` po discussion of explainability
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "These heuristics can bias AI model predictions"
  >
  > "The study demonstrates that offering explainability metrics for predictions to clinicians ... may be ineffective."
  >
  > "heatmaps are woefully inadequate because they are designed to simply make visible model heuristics"
- parafraza: Khera nejde len po presnosti modelu. Ukazuje, ze ani dodatocna explainability vrstva nemusi realne pomoct, ak je postavena na heuristikach, ktore klinik nevie dobre precitat alebo su same skreslene.
- use: pri diskusii, preco nestaci tvrdit, ze model je bezpecny len preto, ze "ukazuje vysvetlenie"

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: clinician-in-the-loop limitation
- locator: PDF p. 2, first full paragraph
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "Having a 'clinician-in-the-loop' overseeing the AI does not overcome the challenges of AI systems failing to provide accurate information"
  >
  > "the bar for clinical decision support using novel technology needs to reflect the challenges likely to be encountered in clinical practice."
- parafraza: Toto je jadro automation bias pointy: samotny clovek v procese neeliminuje riziko. Ak system dava zle podnety, clovek ich moze len legitimizovat, nie opravovat.
- use: velmi silny diskusny anchor pre limity "human oversight" argumentu

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: clinician training and interpretability
- locator: PDF p. 2, middle part of page
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "clinicians will need training on the interpretation of technology outputs"
  >
  > "there is a critical need for methodological innovation and clinician training that goes beyond explainability and provides true interpretability"
- parafraza: Paper neposuva riesenie len na model developerov. Opatrne hovori aj o tom, ze klinici potrebuju trening v tom, ako AI vystupy citat, ako spoznat bias a co povazovat za realnu interpretovatelnost.
- use: pri formulacii buducich implikacii alebo guardrails pre edukacne nasadenie

### Blok 5
- opiera sa o: FDA and downstream harm discussion + closing paragraph
- locator: PDF p. 2, last third of page + closing paragraph
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "The current regulatory evaluation by the FDA is focused on model performance characteristics ... [but] does not fully capture the potential downstream negative consequences"
  >
  > "Clinical decision support tools based on imperfect AI assistive technologies have the potential to result in patient harm because clinicians may trust the output of AI tools over their own judgment."
- parafraza: Regulatory pointa paperu je, ze benchmark model performance nestaci. Klinicky problem vznikne az v interakcii modelu, cloveka a rozhodovania v praxi, kde sa bias moze dalej zosilnovat.
- use: eticky a policy anchor pre diskusiu bezpecnosti AI systemov

## Klucova definicia / vysledok / argument
- Najdolezitejsia veta pre tuto pracu je, ze imperfect assistive AI moze skodit aj vtedy, ked je v procese clovek, ak ten clovek podlahne automatizacnemu biasu a preceni vystup modelu.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Ide o editorial, nie o rozsiahlu systematicku review alebo vlastnu empiricku studiu autorov.
- Paper je velmi silny na framing downstream harm a regulation, menej na podrobne technicke riesenia.
