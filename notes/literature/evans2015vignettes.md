## Kľúčové evidenčné bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: Abstract + opening review rationale
- locator: PDF p. 1-2, Abstract + beginning of article
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Vignette-based methodologies are frequently used to examine judgments and decision-making processes, including clinical judgments made by health professionals. ... As a 'hybrid' of traditional survey and experimental methods, vignette studies can offer aspects of both the high internal validity of experiments and the high external validity of survey research in order to disentangle multiple predictors of clinician behavior. When vignette studies are well designed to test specific questions about judgments and decision-making, they can be highly generalizable to 'real life' behavior."
- parafráza: Evans paper poskytuje základný metodologický argument, prečo môžu byť vignetty použiteľné na štúdium klinického rozhodovania: nejde len o lacnú náhradu reality, ale o hybridný dizajn s potenciálom rozumnej internej aj externej validity.
- use: hlavná opora pre obhajobu vignette metodiky

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: section `Defining features of vignette studies`
- locator: PDF p. 3, `Defining features of vignette studies`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "A vignette is a brief, carefully written description of a person or situation designed to simulate key features of a real world scenario. The content and characteristics of a vignette can be described as consisting of up to three different aspects, based on their function in the study design: (a) experimental aspects, which are systematically manipulated across vignettes ... (b) controlled aspects, which are kept consistent ... and (c) in some cases, contextual aspects, which demonstrate some variation ... to provide verisimilitude."
  >
  > "By parsing the variance of vignette content into these three parts, researchers can strive toward overall vignette equivalence."
- parafráza: Pre praktický dizajn je kľúčové rozlíšiť experimentálne, kontrolované a kontextové prvky vignety. Evans tým dáva presný jazyk, ako premýšľať o tom, čo má byť medzi vignettami menené a čo držané stabilne.
- use: metodická opora pre návrh a audit vignette contentu

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: section `The vignette world and the real world`
- locator: PDF p. 4-5, `The vignette world and the real world`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "First, a vignette must simulate certain aspects of real-world scenarios, often bearing some resemblance to situations encountered by the participant. This simulation function is a facet of construct validity ... Second, vignettes ... are intended to elicit some kind of effect that is hypothesized to exist independently in the real world. ... Third, vignette studies should produce results that generalize to real-world situations."
  >
  > "The more useful question is not whether the 'vignette world' is equivalent to the 'real world,' but rather, whether the mental and behavioral processes used in the study are activated in a manner that is similar to the way in which these processes are used in real life."
- parafráza: Paper posúva diskusiu od naivnej otázky "je vignetta úplne realistická?" k metodicky presnejšej otázke, či spúšťa podobné mentálne a behaviorálne procesy ako reálna situácia.
- use: veľmi silný anchor proti primitívnej kritike, že vignetta "nie je realita"

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: evidence on correspondence with real-life behavior
- locator: PDF p. 5, continuation of `The vignette world and the real world`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The available evidence addressing this issue suggests that participants respond to hypothetical and real-life scenarios in a similar manner. ... family physicians made similar consultation referrals in response to case vignettes as to actual patients ... and rheumatologists showed a nearly perfect correlation (r = .90) in their assessments of real patients and similar 'paper patients'."
  >
  > "In studies with health care providers, clinical vignettes yield findings very similar to those using standardized patients ... In general, clinicians’ perceptions of and responses to vignettes appear to resemble their responses to real life situations."
- parafráza: Evans nezostáva pri teórii, ale uvádza aj empirické indície, že klinici často reagujú na vignetty podobne ako na reálne prípady alebo standardized patients.
- use: sekundárna empirická opora pre validitu vignette-based dizajnu

### Blok 5
- opiera sa o: Table 1 + methodological recommendations
- locator: PDF p. 6, Table 1 + opening `Methodological recommendations`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Vignettes should 1. Derive from the literature and/or clinical experience ... 3. Not be longer than necessary (typically between 50 and 500 words) ... 5. Follow a similar structure and style for all vignettes in the study ... 10. Resemble real people, not a personification of a list of symptoms or behaviors ... 14. Facilitate participant engagement and thinking by including vague or ambiguous elements."
  >
  > "Vignette designs are perhaps the most effective means for minimizing these observer effects ... Regarding internal validity, vignette studies afford researchers control and standardization of vignette presentation, alongside systematic manipulation of key aspects of the vignette."
- parafráza: Table 1 je prakticky použiteľný checklist pre návrh vigniet. Dôležité je najmä, aby sa neopisoval len zoznam symptómov, ale realistická osoba v konzistentnom štýle a primeranej dĺžke.
- use: priamy checklist pre pilot design a quality audit vigniet

## Kľúčová definícia / výsledok / argument
- Dobrá vignette štúdia nestojí na tom, že napodobní celý svet, ale na tom, že kontrolovane aktivuje relevantné rozhodovacie procesy a pritom udrží konzistenciu stimulu.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Ide o metodologický prehľad, nie o jednu konkrétnu validačnú štúdiu pre tvoj presný dizajn.
- Je silný na obhajobu vignette logiky a dizajnové zásady, ale konkrétnu implementáciu aj tak treba zviazať s tvojím obsahom, outcome-mi a účelom nástroja.
