## Kľúčové evidenčné bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: Executive summary
- locator: PDF p. 11-12 / executive summary
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "WHO recognizes that AI holds great promise for the practice of public health and medicine. WHO also recognizes that, to fully reap the benefits of AI, ethical challenges for health care systems, practitioners and beneficiaries of medical and public health services must be addressed. ... There are also potential serious negative consequences if ethical principles and human rights obligations are not prioritized by those who fund, design, regulate or use AI technologies for health."
- parafráza: WHO nezačína zákazom AI, ale podmieneným prísľubom: potenciál je veľký, no len ak sú etika a ľudskoprávne záväzky explicitne prioritizované už pri návrhu, regulácii a používaní.
- use: high-level ethical framing AI v zdravotníctve

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: Executive summary, six principles
- locator: PDF p. 12-16 / executive summary
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The six principles are summarized below ... Protecting human autonomy ... Promoting human well-being and safety and the public interest ... Ensuring transparency, explainability and intelligibility ... Fostering responsibility and accountability ... Ensuring inclusiveness and equity ... Promoting AI that is responsive and sustainable."
- parafráza: WHO explicitne ponúka šesť-princípový etický rámec, ktorý je veľmi použiteľný ako stručná governance kostra aj pre menšie AI zdravotnícke aplikácie.
- use: priamy source of truth pre ethics/guidance sekciu

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: section `5.1 Protect autonomy`
- locator: PDF p. 41-42 / section `5.1 Protect autonomy`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The principle of autonomy requires that any extension of machine autonomy not undermine human autonomy. In the context of health care, this means that humans should remain in full control of health-care systems and medical decisions. ... Respect for autonomy also entails the related duties to protect privacy and confidentiality and to ensure informed, valid consent."
- parafráza: WHO veľmi jasne hovorí, že AI nesmie vytlačiť človeka z rozhodovacieho jadra zdravotnej starostlivosti. Ochrana autonómie zároveň zahŕňa aj privacy, confidentiality a valid informed consent.
- use: opora pre tvrdenie, že tvoja AI aplikácia má zostať pod ľudským dohľadom a nemá robiť autonómne klinické rozhodnutia

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: sections `5.2` + `5.3`
- locator: PDF p. 42-43 / sections `5.2 Promote human well-being...` + `5.3 Ensure transparency...`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "AI technologies should not harm people. They should satisfy regulatory requirements for safety, accuracy and efficacy before deployment, and measures should be in place to ensure quality control and quality improvement. ... Transparency requires that sufficient information be published or documented before the design and deployment of an AI technology. ... AI technologies should be explainable to the extent possible and according to the capacity of those to whom the explanation is directed."
- parafráza: WHO spája bezpečnosť, účinnosť a explainability do jedného praktického nároku: AI sa nemá nasadzovať bez priebežného hodnotenia výkonu, dokumentácie a zrozumiteľného vysvetlenia pre relevantných aktérov.
- use: veľmi dobrá opora pre auditovateľnosť a explainability požiadavky

### Blok 5
- opiera sa o: section `5.4 Foster responsibility and accountability`
- locator: PDF p. 44 / section `5.4`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Although AI technologies perform specific tasks, it is the responsibility of human stakeholders to ensure that they can perform those tasks and that they are used under appropriate conditions. ... When something does go wrong in application of an AI technology, there should be accountability. Appropriate mechanisms should be adopted to ensure questioning by and redress for individuals and groups adversely affected by algorithmically informed decisions."
- parafráza: WHO odmieta predstavu, že za škodu “môže algoritmus sám”. Zodpovednosť ostáva na ľudských stakeholderoch a musia existovať mechanizmy questioning aj redress.
- use: governance argument proti odovzdaniu zodpovednosti modelu

### Blok 6
- opiera sa o: sections `5.5 Ensure inclusiveness and equity` + `5.6 Promote AI that is responsive and sustainable`
- locator: PDF p. 45-46 / sections `5.5` + `5.6`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "AI technologies should not be biased. Bias is a threat to inclusiveness and equity because it represents a departure, often arbitrary, from equal treatment. ... AI developers should ensure that AI data, and especially training data, do not include sampling bias and are therefore accurate, complete and diverse."
  >
  > "Responsiveness requires that designers, developers and users continuously, systematically and transparently examine an AI technology to determine whether it is responding adequately, appropriately and according to communicated expectations and requirements in the context in which it is used."
- parafráza: WHO dáva dôraz na dve pre tvoju tému veľmi praktické veci: bias/data representativeness a priebežné monitorovanie technológie v reálnom kontexte použitia.
- use: etická opora pre bias caution a pre ongoing evaluation namiesto jednorazového “schválenia”

## Kľúčová definícia / výsledok / argument
- AI v zdravotníctve má byť riadená cez ľudskú autonómiu, bezpečnosť, transparentnosť, zodpovednosť, rovnosť a priebežnú responzívnosť; bez toho sa jej prínos ľahko mení na etické a praktické riziko.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Ide o normatívne guidance, nie o empirickú štúdiu.
- Je silné na princípy a governance, ale nie je to zdroj pre konkrétne výkonnostné metriky jednej aplikácie.
