## Kľúčové evidenčné bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: Abstract + opening paragraphs
- locator: PDF p. 1 / journal p. 269, Abstract + Introduction
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Depression is widely acknowledged to be a heterogeneous entity, and the need to further characterize the individual patient who has received this diagnosis in order to personalize the management plan has been repeatedly emphasized. However, the research evidence that should guide this personalization is at present fragmentary, and the selection of treatment is usually based on the clinician’s and/or the patient’s preference and on safety issues, in a trial-and-error fashion, paying little attention to the particular features of the specific case. This may be one of the reasons why the majority of patients with a diagnosis of depression do not achieve remission with the first treatment they receive."
- parafráza: Článok otvára depresiu ako heterogénny syndróm a tvrdí, že bežná klinická prax často funguje trial-and-error štýlom, bez systematického zohľadnenia individuálnych charakteristík pacienta. To je dôležitá opora pre potrebu štruktúrovaného symptom a quality framingu v práci.
- use: klinické ukotvenie, prečo nestačí hovoriť o depresii ako o jednotnej entite

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: discussion of treatment guidelines + routine severity assessment
- locator: PDF p. 1 / journal p. 269, opening paragraphs
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "Treatment guidelines do not help in this respect. They tend to emphasize the severity of the depressive episode as the main or only element on which to base the choice of treatment, but this emphasis is undermined in clinical practice by the lack of a reliable and widely accepted way to evaluate that severity. In fact, the definitions of the various degrees of severity of a depressive episode provided by the DSM-5 and ICD-11 (arguably, somewhat generic, without clear anchor points, not evidence based, and with poor interrater reliability) are often ignored by clinicians."
- parafráza: Autori výslovne problematizujú, že severity je síce v guidelines centrálna, ale jej rutinné klinické uchopenie je slabé, lebo DSM-5 a ICD-11 definície stupňov závažnosti sú generické a majú slabé anchor points aj interrater reliability.
- use: opora pre opatrné narábanie so závažnosťou a pre potrebu konkrétnych symptom anchorov

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: overview of personalization agenda + Table 1
- locator: PDF p. 2 / journal p. 270, paragraph before Table 1 + Table 1
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "The need to develop decision support tools guiding the personalization of depression management has been emphasized, and the point made that these tools should be developed through large observational studies using a comprehensive battery of inexpensive self-report and clinical measures. The present paper aims to describe systematically the salient domains that should be considered in this effort to personalize the treatment of depression (Table 1)."
  >
  > "1. Symptom profile ... 5. Functioning and quality of life ... 8. Antecedent and concomitant psychiatric conditions ... 14. Dysfunctional cognitive schemas."
- parafráza: Personalizácia manažmentu depresie podľa autorov nemá stáť na jednom skóre, ale na širšej batérii klinických a self-report ukazovateľov. Table 1 je prehľadný zdroj 14 domén, ktoré ukazujú, aké široké môže byť klinické charakterizovanie depresie.
- use: rámec pre širší klinický kontext depresie a pre vysvetlenie, že symptom fidelity je len jedna vrstva

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: section `Symptom profile`
- locator: PDF p. 2-3 / journal p. 270-271, section `Symptom profile`
- kontrolný excerpt:
  > "There is some empirical evidence supporting the validity of these lists of symptoms. In fact, in a logistic regression analysis, all nine symptoms listed in the DSM-5 were found to be significant independent predictors of the diagnosis of depression, with the first two symptoms on the list having the highest positive predictive values. In a further analysis, hopelessness about the future, the only ICD-11 symptom not included in the DSM-5, outperformed about half of the DSM-5 symptoms in differentiating depressed from non-depressed subjects. One additional symptom, diminished drive, performed more strongly than almost all of the DSM-5 symptoms. Further items – such as lack of reactivity of mood, anger, irritability, psychic anxiety, and somatic concomitants of anxiety – also discriminated significantly between depressed and non-depressed subjects."
- parafráza: Hoci DSM-5 a ICD-11 symptom lists majú empirickú oporu, autori upozorňujú, že niektoré klinicky významné komponenty depresie presahujú ich jadro. Osobitne zvýrazňujú hopelessness, diminished drive, anxiety a somatic complaints.
- use: keď bude treba ukázať, že depresívna symptomatika je širšia než základný checklist a že interview kvalita má zachytiť aj jemnejší klinický kontext

## Kľúčová definícia / výsledok / argument
- Pre personalizované uvažovanie o depresii treba pacienta klinicky charakterizovať naprieč viacerými doménami, nielen cez samotnú syndrómovú diagnózu alebo jednoduché severity štítky.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Ide o široký klinický prehľad, nie o čistý diagnostický manuál ani psychometrický text.
- Článok je silný na klinické rámovanie heterogenity depresie, ale nie je určený ako jediný anchor pre presné diagnostické kritériá alebo škálové cut-offy.
