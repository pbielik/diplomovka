## Klucove evidencne bloky

### Blok 1
- opiera sa o: Abstract
- locator: PDF p. 1, Abstract
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "The kappa statistic is frequently used to test interrater reliability."
  >
  > "traditionally it was measured as percent agreement... Jacob Cohen critiqued use of percent agreement due to its inability to account for chance agreement."
- parafraza: McHugh dava zrozumitelny vstup do problemu: percent agreement je intuitivny, ale neberie do uvahy nahodnu zhodu, a preto sa v praxi casto siaha po kappa statistike.
- use: kratke metodicke vysvetlenie, preco percent agreement sam osebe nestaci

### Blok 2
- opiera sa o: Percent agreement section
- locator: PDF p. 3, `Percent agreement`
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "In Table 1, the agreement is 80%. This means that 20% of the data collected in the study is erroneous because only one of the raters can be correct when there is disagreement."
  >
  > "This statistic is directly interpreted as the percent of data that are correct."
- parafraza: Silna didakticka pointa paperu je, ze percent agreement ma vyhodu priamej interpretovatelnosti: ak je zhoda 80 %, asi 20 % zaznamov je spornych alebo chybnych. To je uzitocne pri komunikacii reliability aj mimo striktne statistickeho publika.
- use: pri diskusii vecneho vyznamu reliability, nie len samotneho koeficientu

### Blok 3
- opiera sa o: Kappa interpretation section + Table 3
- locator: PDF p. 4, `Cohen's kappa` + Table 3
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "any kappa below 0.60 indicates inadequate agreement among the raters and little confidence should be placed in the study results."
  >
  > "Value of Kappa ... 0.40-0.59 Weak ... 0.60-0.79 Moderate ... 0.80-0.90 Strong ..."
- parafraza: McHugh vedome navrhuje prisnejsiu interpretaciu kappa v zdravotnickom a klinickom vyskume a odmieta citat nizke az stredne hodnoty prilis optimisticky.
- use: opatrny interpretacny anchor pri reporte agreement koeficientov

### Blok 4
- opiera sa o: Interpretation / consequences of low agreement
- locator: PDF p. 5, text around Figure 2
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "When agreement is less than 80%, over 20% of the data being analyzed are erroneous."
  >
  > "When kappa values are below 0.60, the confidence intervals about the obtained kappa are sufficiently wide that one can surmise that about half the data may be incorrect."
- parafraza: Paper tlmoci nizku zhodu do velmi konkretneho dosledku: nizka reliability nie je abstraktny statisticky problem, ale znamena, ze analyzujes podielovo nespolahlive data.
- use: pri zdovodneni, preco je interrater reliability podstatna pre vierohodnost expertneho hodnotenia

### Blok 5
- opiera sa o: Conclusions
- locator: PDF p. 6-7, `Conclusions`
- kontrolny excerpt:
  > "Perhaps the best advice for researchers is to calculate both percent agreement and kappa."
  >
  > "Low levels of interrater reliability are not acceptable in health care or in clinical research..."
- parafraza: Zaverecne odporucanie paperu je pragmaticke: reportovat spolu percent agreement aj kappa a nebagatelizovat nizku zhodu v health research.
- use: pomocny argument pre transparentny reliability reporting vo vysledkoch

## Klucova definicia / vysledok / argument
- McHugh je uzitocny najma ako explanatory anchor: kappa ma korigovat nahodnu zhodu, ale ani ona nema byt citana benevolentne; nizka zhoda priamo oslabuje doveru v dataset a v z neho odvodené zavery.

## Limity alebo pozor
- Paper je o `kappa`, nie o `ICC`. Preto je vhodny na plain-language vysvetlenie agreement logiky a opatrnosti pri interpretacii, ale nie ako primarna autorita pre typologiu `ICC` modelov.
- Ide o kratky didakticky text; na many-raters ordinal setting je silnejsi doplnok `nelson2015manyratersordinal`.
